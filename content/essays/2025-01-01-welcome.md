*The Unicorn Tapestries or the Hunt of the Unicorn ([French](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/French_language): La Chasse à la licorne) is a series of seven [tapestries](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tapestries) made in the [Southern Netherlands](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Southern_Netherlands) around 1495–1505, and now in [The Cloisters](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Cloisters) in New York City. They were possibly designed in [Paris](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Paris) and woven in [Brussels](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Brussels). They depict a group of noblemen and hunters in pursuit of a [unicorn](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Unicorn) through an idealised French landscape. The tapestries were woven in wool, metallic threads, and silk. The vibrant colours, still evident today, were produced from dye plants: [weld](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Reseda_luteola) (yellow), [madder](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rose_madder) (red), and [woad](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Woad) (blue). Wikipedia.*
